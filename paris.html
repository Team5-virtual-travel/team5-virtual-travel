Bofinger

Restaurants serving beer brewed on the premises were all kinds of trendy in the second half of the 19th century. One of the best surviving examples today is Bofinger, located right next to the site of the former Bastille prison. The food is largely inspired by the Alsace region, so think sauerkraut, sausages, ham, and other parts of the pig that are all just fantastic. The Belle Epoque dining room is magical and unapologetically Parisian, with generally fewer tourists than its brasserie brethren.

L’Escargot Montorgueil

It’s all about the cuisine of the Burgundy region at this 19th-century eatery, and that means snails. Buttery and garlicky, it’s basically a given that you’ll order some while sitting inside a dining room trimmed with dark wood and a burgundy-red décor. The restaurant, like many on this list, is a historical monument, and the prices reflect a dining experience in a sumptuous Second Empire establishment -- but it does have a sort of snack menu with various salads, egg dishes, and soups that are more reasonably priced.

Le Train Bleu

When you think of train travel, good food rarely comes to mind, but at the Gare de Lyon train station, this little jewel box of a restaurant might change your mind. Whether you’re heading off to the south of France or just want a classic French meal, Le Train Bleu dining room -- entirely renovated in 2014 -- is the youngest on this list, but one of the most stunning. It’s no wonder Coco Chanel and Brigitte Bardot were regulars here.
